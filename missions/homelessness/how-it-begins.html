Homelessness Categories

How it begins.

The homelessness mission began with a personal encounter, Scripture, and the growing realization that the same visible need may have very different roots.

Here I Am

A calling heard through Isaiah 58.

In 2023, while searching God's word for the meaning of suffering, the words "Here I am" became connected with an encounter with two homeless people in Berlin. One request was refused; the second felt like a chance to respond differently.

Later that day, Isaiah 58 brought the same words into clearer focus: true fasting, loosening the bonds of wickedness, sharing bread with the hungry, and bringing the homeless poor into the house.

Discernment

Need is visible, but the root is often hidden.

Since then, homeless people could no longer remain invisible. Yet the question also became more complicated: it is not possible to help every person in the same way, and what someone asks for may not be what they truly need.

The original page names the complexity directly: homelessness can be entangled with mental health, drug use, gangs, migration, illegal practices, unemployment, family pressure, and other hidden systems.

Categories

Understanding the person before choosing the response.

01

Immediate Need

Hunger, cold, hygiene, safety, loneliness, and exhaustion may need direct compassion before any larger plan can be discussed.

02

Health Burden

Mental health, addiction, trauma, and untreated sickness can shape behavior and limit the kind of support a person can receive.

03

Migration Pressure

Language, legal status, family needs, and unfamiliar systems can leave a person present in the city but disconnected from help.

04

Hidden Exploitation

Some people may be caught near gangs, coercive networks, illegal work, or other structures that make a simple cash response unsafe.

05

Skill Without Access

Some already have skills, a "fishing rod," but do not know where to find stable work, trusted partners, or a path back into dignity.

06

Systemic Gap

Housing, healthcare, employment, and public resources can fail people at the same time, requiring advocacy as well as service.

Response

Give more than fish: help people find where to fish.

The mission therefore moves from one-time aid toward understanding, prayer, education, business opportunity, and social advocacy. Some people need a fishing rod; some already have one but do not know where to catch the fish.

SEiN's response begins with the clear requirement of Micah 6:8: do justice, love kindness, and walk humbly with the Lord.
