Proof of Concept

Compassion Voucher PoC.

New ways of expressing compassion through responsible, restorative giving in Berlin.

Overview

Structured giving for real needs.

Inspired by Mi-Change Voucher from U-turn Homeless Ministries in South Africa, this project explores a better way for anyone in Berlin to help homeless people.

Instead of giving cash, people can buy vouchers that can be used for real needs like food, clothes, hygiene items, or transport. This ensures support reaches what is truly needed and avoids contributing to alcohol or drug use.

The approach starts with testing in a specific area, because homelessness is often regional. Positive results can then be used to approach local organizations and institutes to expand the system across the city.

Current status: reaching out to U-turn Homeless Ministries and planning how to appropriate it to a specific area.

How It Works

Transparent, practical, and dignity-preserving.

01

Buying and Using

Anyone in the city can buy digital or paper vouchers from local organizations, churches, or online. Each voucher can be used at verified partners.

02

Safe and Transparent

Partner shops follow strict no-alcohol/no-drug rules, and every voucher use is logged to show how it is helping and what people need.

03

Support Pathways

Those giving vouchers can also encourage homeless people to access programs like counseling, skill-building, rehabilitation, or longer-term support.

Why a PoC?

Testing a smarter way to give.

01

Core Questions

Can giving shift from spontaneous cash donations to a structured and compassionate system? Can it reduce street cash that fuels alcohol and drug use?

02

Funding Needs

Funding supports communication, system development, vendor onboarding, monitoring and evaluation, coordination, and staffing.

03

Evidence

The PoC asks whether the system can create pathways from immediate help to longer-term support and inform future city-wide programs or policies.

Impact Goals

From impulsive relief to thoughtful support.

This PoC aims to encourage responsible giving, improve access to real needs, reduce cash flow into alcohol and drug use, gather insight, and create respectful, empowering interactions for homeless people.

Inspired by the U-turn Homeless Ministries model, the Compassion Voucher System tests this idea in a European urban context.
