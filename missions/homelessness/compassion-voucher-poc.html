Proof of Concept

Compassion Voucher PoC.

Responsible giving for real needs in Berlin.

Overview

Structured giving for real needs.

Inspired by Mi-Change Voucher from U-turn Homeless Ministries in South Africa, this project explores vouchers for food, clothes, hygiene items, or transport instead of direct cash.

The approach starts with a local test area and can expand through partner organizations if results are strong.

Current status: reaching out to U-turn Homeless Ministries and planning how to adapt the model locally.

How It Works

Transparent, practical, and dignity-preserving.

01

Buying and Using

Anyone in the city can buy digital or paper vouchers from local organizations, churches, or online. Each voucher can be used at verified partners.

02

Safe and Transparent

Partner shops follow strict no-alcohol/no-drug rules, and every voucher use is logged to show how it is helping and what people need.

03

Support Pathways

Those giving vouchers can also encourage homeless people to access programs like counseling, skill-building, rehabilitation, or longer-term support.

Why a PoC?

Testing a smarter way to give.

01

Core Questions

Can giving shift from spontaneous cash to structured compassion? Can it reduce cash that fuels addiction?

02

Funding Needs

Funding supports communication, system development, vendor onboarding, monitoring, coordination, and staffing.

03

Evidence

The PoC tests whether vouchers can connect immediate help with longer-term support.

Impact Goals

From impulsive relief to thoughtful support.

This PoC aims to improve access to real needs, reduce harmful cash flow, gather insight, and create respectful interactions.
