How it begins

A Christian education, business, and social initiative for Berlin's homeless.

This initiative offers a holistic, faith-based response to the homelessness crisis in Berlin, where increasing social needs confront limited government resources.

Inspired by the legacy of George Müller's faith and William Wilberforce's advocacy, the initiative combines ministry, Christian social education, business creation, and policy engagement to restore lives and renew communities.

Applied Method

Understanding, education and business, social advocacy.

01

Understanding

Aim to understand every aspect of homelessness—from the causes that lead people into homelessness, to their current mindset and behaviors, and how they may be categorized into different groups. While research can be conducted from a macro perspective, this is also a personal reminder: each individual is created in the image of God and is uniquely made.

02

Education & Business

Discipleship-first education that empowers individuals with both spiritual grounding and practical skills for employment and independent living. Quality matters far more than quantity—a surrendered heart is worth more than many that are double-minded. Social businesses are launched not just for income, but to restore dignity and community through meaningful, faith-filled work.

03

Social Advocacy

Addressing systemic gaps—especially in housing and healthcare—by building partnerships and advocating for policies that reflect justice and compassion. Rooted in truth and prayer, this effort seeks to ensure the most vulnerable are seen, heard, and supported.

Actions

From archive to proof of concept.

01

Awakening Hope

This project unites Berlin's international and local churches to confront the city's homelessness crisis with faith and compassion. By fostering understanding, empowerment, and collaboration across cultures, it seeks to restore dignity, build bridges between churches and the streets, and inspire a city-wide movement of hope and community transformation.

02

Knowledge Hub

Inspired by Crisis homelessness charity and a dear friend in Portugal who regularly supports the homeless by printing and sharing helpful information, this project seeks to create a centralized Knowledge Hub for people in vulnerable situations and those who want to help practically.

03

A Step Forward PoC

The project seeks support from businesses and partner organizations to develop a platform that empowers homeless individuals with practical skills and real-world opportunities to earn income and reinvest in their communities.

04

Compassion Voucher PoC

Inspired by Mi-Change Voucher from U-turn Homeless Ministries in South Africa, this project explores a better way for anyone in Berlin to help homeless people through vouchers for real needs like food, clothes, hygiene items, or transport.

05

To Be List

The mission is not a checklist of tasks, but a living “to-be” list: a description of what SEiN is becoming as faith, knowledge, and action are brought together.
