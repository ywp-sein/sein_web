About SEiN

Archive social issues through Christ.

SEiN exists to confront social issues without delay, archive them with care, and respond through the transformation of individual hearts.

Calling

Whenever an issue arises, we shall confront it without delay. The commitment is not to the divisions of left or right, liberal or conservative, nor to any ideology or system. The allegiance is to justice and compassion.

Mission

The poor are fed, injustice is opposed, and the weak are protected. Wherever these duties are neglected, change is not optional; it is imperative. That change begins with conscience, courage, love, and the transformation of each individual heart.

Why SEiN?

A name, a mission, and a “to-be” calling.

The name “SEiN” emerged in a conversation with someone about the word “to be” in Latin. Later, the Lord confirmed that this is the name He chose for my mission. SEiN stands for Save Everyone in Need, embodying both the mission and motivation God has placed on my heart.

“To be or not to be,” or in German, “Sein oder Nichtsein”—this is not a question. If this truly comes from God for His glory, then all His promises are a firm YES and Amen. He who calls me is faithful, and He will definitely do it.

I believe, therefore I speak in this way: This project will not be a list of to-do tasks but rather a description of a “to-be” mission.

Inspirations

Faithful dependence and public courage.

SEiN is shaped by examples such as George Müller’s faith and William Wilberforce’s advocacy: dependence on God, courage before public injustice, and practical love for people who are easily overlooked.

The archive exists so that prayer, observation, research, and action can remain connected. Information is not the same as knowledge, and knowledge is not the same as wisdom.
