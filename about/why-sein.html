Why SEiN

A name and a “to-be” way.

SEiN begins with the conviction that social issues must be confronted without delay, but that faithful change begins with transformed hearts.

Why SEiN?

Save Everyone in Need as a way of being.

The name “SEiN” emerged in a conversation with someone about the word “to be” in Latin. Later, the Lord confirmed that this is the name He chose for my mission. SEiN stands for Save Everyone in Need, embodying both the mission and motivation God has placed on my heart.

“To be or not to be,” or in German, “Sein oder Nichtsein”—this is not a question. If this truly comes from God for His glory, then all His promises are a firm YES and Amen. He who calls me is faithful, and He will definitely do it.

I believe, therefore I speak in this way: This project will not be a list of to-do tasks but rather a description of a “to-be” mission.

Inspirations

Faithful dependence and public courage.

Faith in Action: Inspired by George Müller and William Wilberforce.

George Müller demonstrated unwavering faith by providing for orphans solely through prayer and trust in God’s provision, showing that faith-led action can meet real and lasting needs. This inspires a commitment to trust God daily while developing sustainable care and business opportunities for the homeless.

William Wilberforce, motivated by biblical justice, devoted his life to ending the British transatlantic slave trade through persistent advocacy. His legacy encourages both compassionate service to the homeless and bold engagement with government and society to pursue systemic change, even in the face of financial or political obstacles.

A call to churches: embrace both compassionate service and courageous advocacy.

A call to society: partner with faith initiatives to address homelessness holistically.
